COMPANY POLICY DOCUMENT
========================

Acme Corporation — Employee Handbook (2025 Edition)

1. LEAVE POLICY
---------------

1.1 Annual Leave
All full-time employees are entitled to 24 days of paid annual leave per calendar year. Leave accrues at 2 days per month of service. Unused leave may be carried forward up to a maximum of 10 days into the next calendar year. Any leave beyond the carry-forward limit will be forfeited.

1.2 Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per year. A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding 2 consecutive working days. Unused sick leave cannot be carried forward or encashed.

1.3 Maternity & Paternity Leave
Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave. Male employees are entitled to 2 weeks of paid paternity leave. These must be availed within 6 months of the child's birth.

1.4 Public Holidays
The company observes 12 public holidays per year as per the regional calendar. The list is published at the beginning of each calendar year.


2. WORK FROM HOME POLICY
--------------------------

2.1 Eligibility
All employees who have completed their probation period (90 days) are eligible for work from home arrangements. Interns and probationary employees must work from the office.

2.2 Remote Work Days
Employees may work from home up to 3 days per week. The remaining 2 days must be spent in the office for collaboration and team meetings. Managers may adjust this ratio based on project needs.

2.3 Core Hours
Regardless of location, all employees must be available during core hours: 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM in their local time zone. Employees are expected to maintain a stable internet connection and a professional working environment.

2.4 Equipment
The company provides a laptop and necessary peripherals. Employees are responsible for maintaining a suitable home office setup including a desk, chair, and reliable internet connection (minimum 50 Mbps).

2.5 Communication
Employees working from home must be reachable via Slack, email, and video conferencing tools during core hours. Response time should not exceed 30 minutes during working hours.


3. COMPENSATION & BENEFITS
----------------------------

3.1 Salary Reviews
Performance-based salary reviews are conducted annually in April. Increments range from 5% to 20% based on performance ratings and market benchmarks.

3.2 Bonus
An annual performance bonus of up to 15% of the annual CTC is awarded based on individual and company performance. Bonuses are paid in May.

3.3 Health Insurance
The company provides comprehensive health insurance covering the employee, spouse, and up to 2 dependent children. Coverage includes hospitalization, outpatient care, dental, and vision benefits up to $50,000 per year.

3.4 Retirement Benefits
The company contributes 12% of the basic salary to the employee's retirement fund. Employees may opt for an additional voluntary contribution of up to 5%.


4. CODE OF CONDUCT
--------------------

4.1 Professional Behavior
All employees are expected to maintain the highest standards of professional conduct. Discrimination, harassment, and bullying are strictly prohibited and will result in immediate disciplinary action.

4.2 Confidentiality
Employees must not disclose proprietary information, trade secrets, or client data to unauthorized parties. A Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) is signed upon joining.

4.3 Conflict of Interest
Employees must disclose any potential conflicts of interest, including outside employment, investments, or relationships that could influence their work decisions.

4.4 Use of Company Resources
Company resources including equipment, software, and internet access are to be used primarily for business purposes. Limited personal use is acceptable but must not interfere with work responsibilities.


5. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT
---------------------------

5.1 Goal Setting
At the beginning of each quarter, employees and their managers set SMART goals aligned with departmental and company objectives.

5.2 Reviews
Formal performance reviews are conducted semi-annually in June and December. Continuous feedback is encouraged through monthly one-on-one meetings.

5.3 Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
Employees receiving a "Below Expectations" rating will be placed on a 90-day Performance Improvement Plan with clear milestones and regular check-ins.


6. TRAINING & DEVELOPMENT
---------------------------

6.1 Learning Budget
Each employee receives an annual learning budget of $2,000 for courses, certifications, books, and conferences.

6.2 Internal Training
The company conducts monthly knowledge-sharing sessions and quarterly skill workshops. Attendance is encouraged but not mandatory.

6.3 Mentorship Program
New employees are paired with a senior mentor for their first 6 months. The mentor provides guidance on technical skills, company culture, and career development.
